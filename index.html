Acts

STATUTORY ACT IN RELATION TO MANAGEMENT OF FINANCES

  • PUBLIC FUNDS MANAGEMENT ACT NO 1. OF 1999

    The act aims to secure transparency, accountability and sound management in public institutions and organizations funded by government and state organs. Furthermore, the Acts dictates regulation of financial management to ensure that all revenue, expenditure, assets and liabilities are managed efficiently and effectively to provide for the responsibilities of persons entrusted with financial managements.

  • SOUTH AFRICAN SCHOOLS ACT 84 OF 1996 (AS AMMENDED BY ACT 15 OF 2011)

    The governing or board members must keep proper financial records of the school (or any organization funded by the state) activities. The executive committee of the SGB, finance administrative clerk and the principal must ensure that the books are reconciled every month by preparing bank reconciliation statements. Management accounts should be prepared on a quarterly basis for presentation to the executive committee or to the entire members of the SGB or board members. At the end of the financial year, accounts must be finalized for submission to a registered independent auditor or alternately a registered accounting officer.
    In consideration the prescripts of PFMA Act No. 1 of 1999 and SASA Act No. 84 of 1996, it is advisable that a registered bookkeeper or certified accountant in practice be appointed to ensure that books of entries are reconciled every month or quarterly.
